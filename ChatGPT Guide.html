AI Tools Pilot Programme

Staff Guidelines & Account Usage Policy
ELP Centres — Auckland South & Auckland Central  |  Version 1.0  |  March 2026  |  Review: June 2026
⚠️ Important: These guidelines exist to protect our accounts from suspension and to ensure AI tools are used responsibly. All staff who access shared accounts must read and sign the acknowledgement in Section 12 before use.

1. Purpose of This Pilot

This pilot introduces two AI tools across Auckland South and Auckland Central ELP Centres on a shared-account basis. The aim is to explore how AI can support teaching, administration, and content creation — before making decisions about wider investment.

Tools in scope:

  • ChatGPT Plus — one account per centre (2 total). For text-based tasks such as lesson planning, email drafting, and summarising documents.
  • ElevenLabs Creator — one account shared across both centres (centrally managed). For generating audio content such as listening exercises and voice-overs.

Pilot duration: 3 months (March – May 2026), followed by a review to decide on continuation or upgrade.

4. Scheduling — When to Use the Accounts

To avoid simultaneous logins — which can trigger platform security systems and risk account suspension — each centre follows a structured usage schedule for ChatGPT Plus.

⚠️ Critical rule: NEVER log into the same ChatGPT account from two different devices or locations at the same time. This is one of the most common causes of account suspension on shared accounts.

🕗 Morning Block — 8:00am–12:30pm

Teaching staff: lesson planning, resource creation, generating reading/listening tasks, vocabulary exercises.

🕐 Afternoon Block — 12:30pm–4:30pm

Admin & teachers: drafting emails, reports, meeting summaries, marking feedback templates, parent communications.

🕔 After Hours — 4:30pm–7:00pm

Available on request for planned tasks. Must be pre-booked with the AI Champion at least one day in advance.

📅 How to Book

Use the shared Google Calendar (link from Centre Manager). Book a 30–60 min slot. If your slot overlaps with another booking, do not log in.

ElevenLabs scheduling: Submit audio content requests to the AI Champion at least 48 hours in advance using the request form. The Champion produces the audio during their designated session and delivers the file to the requester. Do not attempt to log in directly.

5. What You Are Allowed to Do ✅

ChatGPT Plus — Permitted Uses
  • Drafting and improving lesson plans and unit outlines
  • Creating reading comprehension texts, gap-fill exercises, and vocabulary tasks
  • Generating discussion questions, roleplay scenarios, and writing prompts
  • Drafting professional emails, newsletters, and internal communications
  • Summarising meeting notes or long documents (no student data involved)
  • Creating rubrics, marking feedback templates, and assessment criteria
  • Translating or simplifying texts for different ESOL levels
  • Brainstorming ideas for classroom activities or programme themes
  • Researching general information or professional development topics
  • Proofreading and improving your own writing
  • Creating differentiated versions of tasks for different learner levels
  • Generating example sentences, dialogues, or model answers for student reference
ElevenLabs Creator — Permitted Uses
  • Generating voice-overs for listening exercises and classroom audio tasks
  • Creating pronunciation examples for specific sounds, words, or phrases
  • Producing audio versions of reading passages for accessibility
  • Creating natural-sounding audio in multiple accents or dialects for ESOL listening practice
  • Narrating centre communications or short instructional videos
  • Generating audio for digital learning resources or student handouts

6. What You Are NOT Allowed to Do ❌

The following are strictly prohibited for both ChatGPT Plus and ElevenLabs:
  • Student personal data: Never enter a student's real name, date of birth, contact details, visa status, health information, enrolment number, or any other personally identifiable information into any AI tool.
  • Sharing login credentials: Never share the account username or password with anyone outside authorised Champions — not via text, email, WhatsApp, Post-it note, or verbally to non-authorised staff.
  • Simultaneous logins: Never log into an account at the same time as another person, even from within the same centre. Always check the booking calendar first.
  • Using VPNs or proxies: Never use a VPN, proxy, or anonymising service when accessing these accounts. This triggers security flags and can result in immediate suspension.
  • Non-work use: Do not use the accounts for personal tasks, shopping, entertainment, or creative writing unrelated to your role at the centre.
  • Generating misleading or false content: Do not create fake testimonials, fabricated quotes, misleading marketing claims, or content that misrepresents the centre or its programmes.
  • Cloning real people's voices: Do not use ElevenLabs to replicate or clone the voice of any real person — student, staff member, or public figure — without their explicit written consent.
  • Uploading copyrighted material: Do not paste in full published texts, book chapters, or copyrighted curricula for AI to reproduce or rephrase for redistribution.
  • Sharing AI-generated content without review: Do not distribute AI-generated materials to students or external parties without first reviewing them for accuracy and appropriateness.
  • Bypassing these guidelines: Do not attempt to create personal accounts under the organisation's name, or use accounts in ways that would violate these guidelines or the platform's Terms of Service.

7. Data Privacy & Confidentiality

Both ChatGPT and ElevenLabs may use your inputs to improve their systems unless you opt out. Staff must follow these rules at all times:

  • No student data — not even anonymised data if it could reasonably identify a student.
  • No internal HR or payroll information — staff details, salaries, performance notes, or disciplinary records must never be entered.
  • No confidential organisational data — strategic plans, funding agreements, or contractual documents must not be inputted.
  • Opt out of model training — the account owner must ensure the "Improve the model for everyone" setting is turned OFF in ChatGPT settings (Settings → Data Controls). Check this monthly, as it can reset after updates.
  • ElevenLabs voice data — do not upload audio recordings of real people's voices without written consent. Use the platform's synthetic voice library instead.
  • General principle — treat every input as if it could be read by a third party, because it may be stored on overseas servers subject to different privacy laws.
🔒 Privacy reminder: New Zealand's Privacy Act 2020 applies to how we handle student and staff information. Entering personal information into offshore AI systems without consent may constitute a privacy breach. When in doubt, leave it out.

9. Quality Control — Always Check AI Outputs

AI tools can produce confident-sounding but inaccurate, biased, or inappropriate content. You are responsible for everything you deliver or publish. Before using any AI-generated content with students or in official communications:

  • Read everything carefully before use. Never copy-paste without reviewing the full output.
  • Verify facts — AI can fabricate statistics, dates, names, and references. Cross-check any factual claims with a reliable source.
  • Check cultural appropriateness — AI may produce content that is culturally insensitive, stereotyping, or irrelevant to your students' backgrounds and contexts.
  • Check language level — AI frequently misjudges appropriate ESOL levels. Always review and adjust vocabulary, sentence length, and complexity before use.
  • Check for bias — AI systems can reflect biases present in their training data. Review content for gender, cultural, or other bias before distributing to students.
  • Take responsibility — do not present AI-generated materials as entirely your own original work without disclosure where required by organisational policy.
  • Audio check — always listen to ElevenLabs audio in full before distributing. Listen for mispronunciations, unnatural pauses, incorrect stress, or inappropriate tone.

10. Feedback & Pilot Evaluation

This pilot only has value if we gather meaningful data. All staff who use the tools are asked to contribute to the evaluation:

  • Usage Log: Record every session in the shared Google Sheet (link from Centre Manager). Log the date, task type, approximate time spent, and a brief note on whether the output was useful.
  • Monthly Feedback Form: A short 5-minute form will be sent by the AI Champion at the end of each month. Please complete it promptly.
  • Ad-hoc feedback: Flag any concerns, surprises, near-misses, or policy questions to your AI Champion as they arise — don't wait for the monthly form.

At the end of the 3-month pilot, the Regional Coordinator will review usage data and staff feedback to decide whether to continue with shared accounts, expand access, or upgrade to a proper team/business plan.
